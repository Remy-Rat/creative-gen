# GLAMRDiP Video Ad Editing Guidelines

Living document - updated as we learn what works and what doesn't across ad builds.

---

## 1. Opening / First 3 Seconds

- Never open with a black screen. Meta autoplay means the first frame IS the ad - black = scroll past.
- Never open with a text-only screen. Text on its own doesn't stop thumbs. Lead with movement, a face, or a product shot.
- The hook must be visual first, text second. Something needs to be happening on screen before any text appears.
- Strong openers: a hand dipping into powder, a close-up result reveal, someone reacting with genuine surprise, an eye-catching product toss/catch.


## 2. Subtitles

- Keep subtitles anchored in one consistent position throughout the ad. Bottom centre is the default. Do not move them between cuts.
- Reference: RESTOCKED.mp4 handled subtitles well - consistent placement, readable, didn't fight the visuals.
- Use bold, high-contrast text (white with dark outline/shadow) sized for mobile viewing.
- Subtitle chunks should be 3-5 words max per group so they're scannable at speed.
- Don't let subtitles overlap with safe zones (top 15% for platform UI, bottom 10% for CTA buttons on Meta).
- Subtitles should appear and disappear in sync with the spoken words - not early, not late.


## 3. Text Overlays (Non-Subtitle)

- Text overlays (callouts, stats, product names) can be more creative with placement - top left, mid right, stacked centre, etc.
- But they should still feel intentional, not random. Each placement should relate to what's on screen.
- RESTOCKED.mp4 is the benchmark - it used varied placements that complemented the visuals rather than competing with them.
- Avoid putting text over faces or key product details.
- Keep text on screen long enough to read twice at normal speed (roughly 1.5-2s minimum for short phrases).


## 4. UGC Clips + Voiceover

- Do NOT show a person visibly talking to camera while an AI voiceover (or different person's voice) is playing. It creates a jarring mismatch where the viewer can see lips moving but hears a different voice - feels like the person is trying to speak but is muted.
- When using AI voiceover: cut to B-roll, product shots, results, or application footage instead. Only show faces when they're reacting, smiling, or displaying nails - not actively speaking.
- When using the UGC creator's own voice: showing their face talking is great and authentic. Cut away to B-roll periodically to keep it dynamic, then snap back to their face for key moments.
- Rule of thumb: if the audio doesn't match the mouth, don't show the mouth.


## 5. UGC-Led Ads (Real Voice)

- Real customer audio is more authentic than AI voice for testimonial-style ads.
- Multi-voice mashups work well - stitch 2-4 different people's best moments to cover different selling points (price, ease, health, results).
- Show the person's face for the hook and CTA. Use B-roll during the middle sections to keep visual variety.
- Subtitles are essential when using real UGC audio since recording quality varies.


## 6. Pacing and Cut Rhythm

- For Meta ads (15-45s), aim for a new visual every 1.5-3 seconds. Holding a single shot longer than 4s risks losing attention.
- Exception: face-to-camera UGC moments can hold 3-5s when the person's energy carries it.
- Vary the visual energy - don't put all fast cuts together or all slow shots together. Alternate.
- End with a clear visual CTA moment (product shot, brand logo, or the person directly addressing the viewer).


## 7. Aspect Ratio and Framing

- Primary format: 9:16 portrait (1080x1920) for Meta Reels/Stories and TikTok.
- Secondary: 4:5 (1080x1350) for Meta feed.
- When using landscape (16:9) source clips, crop to portrait rather than letterboxing. Centre the subject.
- Always check that faces and key products aren't cut off after cropping.


## 8. Audio

- Background music should sit well underneath voice, not compete with it.
- When using UGC audio: normalise levels so volume doesn't jump between different speakers in a mashup.
- Silence or near-silence at the start is fine if the visual hook is strong enough, but don't let it last more than 1 second.
